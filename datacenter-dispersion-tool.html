Part 3 · 24 Points

The Tool

Tool Description

This tool estimates uncontrolled particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) emission rates from two power plant alternatives — a pulverized coal (dry-bottom) plant and a natural gas combined-cycle (CCGT) plant — sized to meet a user-specified AI data center power demand.

The tool requires one primary user input: the data center's electrical power demand in megawatts (MW). Using fixed literature-based thermal efficiencies (heat rates) from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), the tool converts power demand into plant heat input (MMBtu/hr) for each plant type. For coal, the user may also adjust the coal ash content (weight %), which directly affects the PM emission factor per EPA AP-42 Section 1.1.

The modeling approach applies EPA AP-42 emission factors — the standard EPA compilation of air pollutant emission rates — to compute mass emission rates (lb/hr and g/s) for each plant and each pollutant. For coal, PM10 is computed as 7 × ash fraction × heat input, and PM2.5 as 25% of PM10, per AP-42 Table 1.1-4 size distribution data for dry-bottom PC boilers. For natural gas, all PM is assumed less than 1 µm in diameter (no coarse mineral ash fraction), so PM10 = PM2.5 = 0.0075 lb/MMBtu per AP-42 Section 1.4.

Outputs are presented as a side-by-side table and a proportional bar chart, allowing direct comparison of PM emission rates under identical power demand conditions. A coal-to-gas ratio is computed for each pollutant to quantify the relative emissions difference. The user should interpret these outputs as uncontrolled mass emission rates — the amount of PM exiting the stack before any control technology (e.g., baghouse, electrostatic precipitator) is applied. Future versions of this tool will add control efficiencies, Gaussian dispersion modeling, and MCDA comparison.
