MODEL VALIDATION CHECKLIST

✅
Gaussian Plume Equation: Implements C(x,y,z) = Q/(π·σ_y·σ_z·u) · exp(−y²/2σ_y²) · [exp(−(z−H_eff)²/2σ_z²) + exp(−(z+H_eff)²/2σ_z²)]. Ground-level (z=0) simplification applied correctly.
✅
Briggs Plume Rise: Momentum and buoyancy flux (F_b, F_m) computed from stack exit velocity, diameter, temperature, and ambient conditions. Final effective height H_eff = H_stack + Δh.
✅
Pasquill-Gifford σ parameterization: Briggs (1973) open-country σ_y and σ_z formulas by stability class. Valid range 0.1–10 km downwind.
✅
Emission rates: Q derived from fuel consumption via heat rate × power demand. PM₁₀/PM₂.₅ emission factors from EPA AP-42 Table 1.1-3 (bituminous coal) and AP-42 Ch. 3.1 (natural gas). Baghouse efficiency 99.5% applied to uncontrolled coal factor.
✅
NAAQS comparison: Hourly concentrations compared to annual PM₂.₅ NAAQS (9 µg/m³) and 24-hr standard (35 µg/m³) per EPA 2024 revision. Note: model output represents a snapshot, not annual average; interpret as screening-level estimate.
⚠️
Flat terrain only: Model assumes flat, unobstructed terrain. No building downwash, terrain-following, or complex topography. Real-world deployment should use AERMOD or CALPUFF for regulatory analysis.
⚠️
Steady-state assumption: Gaussian plume is steady-state; does not capture fumigation, calm conditions (u < 0.5 m/s), or nighttime stable layer breakup.
⚠️
PM₂.₅ fraction: For coal, PM₂.₅ ≈ 70% of PM₁₀ (EPA AP-42 adjustment). For NG, PM₂.₅ ≈ 90% of filtered PM. Baghouse removes PM₁₀ with high efficiency; PM₂.₅ penetration ~5%.
❌
Secondary PM not included: SO₂ and NOₓ secondary aerosol formation not modeled. Coal plants produce significant secondary PM₂.₅. This is a conservative underestimate for coal alternatives.
❌
No deposition or chemical transformation: Dry/wet deposition and chemical decay not implemented. Results overestimate concentrations at very long distances.
